Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation.

They set the trajectory for integrated circuits and semiconductors.

Learn More →

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

The 4004 packed 2,300 transistors, running at 740 kHz; designed for a calculator, it sparked modern computing.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086, establishing the x86 architecture.

Introduced 16-bit processing and became the foundation for the x86 family.

1985

386 Processor

32-bit architecture ushers in new performance and multitasking.

Enabled advanced multitasking and cemented the x86 standard on PCs.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Highest annual operational emissions; trend reverses with abatement and renewables.

Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and efficiency thereafter.

2020

RISE Strategy

RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) and 2030 goals.

Aligns innovation with sustainability and inclusivity across operations and supply chain.

Learn More →

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Commitment to net-zero Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2040.

Builds on years of environmental initiatives; pledges carbon neutrality.

Learn More →

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity worldwide.

Partnerships and long-term procurement enabled this milestone.

Learn More →

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit with suppliers and leaders.

Focus on breakthrough tech, policy alignment, and sustainable manufacturing.
